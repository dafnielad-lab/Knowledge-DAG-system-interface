$\sin(\alpha + \beta) = \cos\!\left(\tfrac{\pi}{2} - (\alpha + \beta)\right) = \cos\!\left((\tfrac{\pi}{2} - \alpha) - \beta\right)$

$= \cos\!\left(\tfrac{\pi}{2} - \alpha\right)\cos\beta + \sin\!\left(\tfrac{\pi}{2} - \alpha\right)\sin\beta = \sin\alpha\cos\beta + \cos\alpha\sin\beta$. $\blacksquare$
